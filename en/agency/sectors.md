# Deployment Sectors and Operational Needs

The development of the CARTIO Project addresses the need for data standardization in three areas where infrastructure collapse prevents the coordinated action of response teams:

---

## 1. Civil Disaster Response (Humanitarian Aid)
In large-scale natural disasters (such as severe floods, earthquakes, or prolonged power blackouts):

*   **Immediate Integration (Day Zero)**: Allows civil agencies, NGOs, and health organizations to start exchanging records interoperably without needing to rebuild complex physical communication networks.
*   **Identification under Conventions**: Proper and secure registration of medical and civil rescue teams, ensuring their identification in accordance with international rules.
*   **Humanitarian Logistics**: Assistance in the dynamic reconfiguration of rescue routes and identification of isolated supply depots.

---

## 2. Support for Sovereignty and Border Surveillance
In protection and humanitarian aid operations conducted by the Brazilian Army:

*   **Operations in Remote Areas**: Facilitates identity management and access control in advanced bases along river or land borders without stable commercial satellite coverage.
*   **Radio Communications**: Synchronization of military and partner agent credentials through tactical VHF/UHF radio transceivers subject to noise and environmental interference.

---

## 3. Critical Infrastructure Resilience
Guaranteeing operation in public utility services:

*   **Emergency Physical Access Control**: Continuous operation of biometric readers, turnstiles, and security credentials at power substations or water reservoirs, even if external networks are down.
