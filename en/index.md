<section class="portal-section history-section">
<h2>FOUNDATION AND DEFENSE CONTEXT</h2>
<div class="history-grid">
<div class="history-text">
<h3>The Strategic Role of the Army and IME</h3>
<p>The Brazilian Army plays a fundamental role in supporting the population in natural disaster scenarios, guaranteeing sovereignty, and monitoring land borders. In these extreme crisis situations, the partial or total loss of commercial communications is a constant.</p>
<p>It is at this threshold of high entropy that the <strong>CARTIO Project</strong> fits in: proposing an open, lightweight, and autonomous standard to guarantee access control and identity flow without depending on cloud connectivity or robust backhauls.</p>
</div>
<div class="history-text">
<h3>History and Scientific Tradition</h3>
<p>The project's research is developed at the <strong>Military Institute of Engineering (IME)</strong>, whose origin dates back to the <em>Real Academy of Artillery, Fortification, and Design</em>, founded in <strong>1792</strong>, configuring the first engineering school of the Americas and the third in the world.</p>
<p>This heritage of scientific engineering guides the project, combining classic computing with network survivability to provide solutions that support decision-making when conventional means fail.</p>
</div>
</div>
</section>
